Stage II: The Variable Validator (Application)

Goal: Correctly identify the core components of acontrolled experiment using a lab manual example. All three must be correct to proceed.

Original Lab Manual Scenario:

A plant biologist conducts an experiment based on thehypothesis: 'Tomato plants grow taller when given Fertilizer X.'

She uses 20 identical tomato plants. Group A (theExperimental Group) is given the standard amount of water plus a single dose of Fertilizer X. Group B (theControl Group) is given only the standard amount of water. All plants are kept in the same greenhouse with the same soil, temperature, and light exposure. After four weeks, the researcher measures the height of every plant.

1. Independent Variable (IV): What factor is beingmanipulated by the researcher?

The height of the plants.
The amount of light, soil, and temperature.
The presence or absence of Fertilizer X.

2. Dependent Variable (DV): What factor is beingmeasured or observed?

The height of the plants.
The presence or absence of Fertilizer X.
The amount of water given to the plants.

3. Control Variables: Which factors are being keptconstant (and are essential for making the conclusion valid)?

The amount of water and the height of the plants.
The soil, temperature, and light exposure.
The fertilizer and the height of the plants.

Stage III: The Flaw Detective (MCAT Evaluation)

Goal: Analyze a complex, observational study to identify flaws, confounding variables, and correct reasoning.

MCAT-Style Scenario (Non-Ideal Research):

A university study surveyed 1000 freshmen, asking about their diet and sleep habits. The data showed astrong correlation between students who consistently ate breakfast every morning and those who reported getting 8 hours of sleep. Furthermore, these students had asignificantly higher average GPA than students who often skipped breakfast and reported less sleep.

The researchers concluded: "Eating breakfast and sleeping 8 hourscauses students to achieve a higher GPA."

1. Evaluate the Conclusion: The researcher's conclusion that breakfast/sleep *causes* a higher GPA is flawed. Why?

The study was conducted on freshmen, which is not representative of all students.
This was anobservational study and cannot prove causation, onlycorrelation.
The number of students (1000) was too small to draw a conclusion.

2. Identify the Confound: Which of the following is the most likelyconfounding variable—a third factor that causes both the healthy habits AND the high GPA?

The size of the university campus.
Students with higher levels ofdiscipline and time management (traits that lead to a structured schedule and good study habits).
The color of the highlighters they use.

3. Propose a Fix (Open Response): Briefly describe the most critical requirement for atrue controlled experiment to *prove* a causal link between breakfast/sleep and GPA. (What must be done to the students?)
