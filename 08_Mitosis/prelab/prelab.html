Pre-Lab: Plan & Prime

Week 1: Science as a Way of Knowing

Big picture: learn how scientists think, measure, and see. Build the skills you’ll use all semester.

Your Objectives This Week

Understand

  • Differentiate science, pseudoscience, and testable claims.
  • Define hypothesis, theory, law; identify variables (independent, dependent, controls).

Measure

  • Use metric prefixes fluently; convert units in exponential form.
  • Compute microscope field diameters for (4×, 10×, & 40×) lenses and estimate specimen size.

Observe

  • Operate a compound LM safely; adjust light and focus appropriately.
  • Recognize size differences between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells.

Instructions

  1. Open Scientific Method Terms and study the terms related to the scientific method, then do the Science Detective activity to practice hypotheses and variables. (25–35 min)
  2. Read Pseudoscience & Claims for three (3) examples of everyday non‑testable claims. (30 min)
  3. Work through Metrics: prefixes, conversions, & exponential form (e.g., 6.3 × 10-6). (20–25 min)
  4. Go to Microscope Mastery: Without the microscope, biology would be blind to the cellular world that drives health, disease, and life itself. Every biology student must master microscope skills because accurate observation and measurement at the cellular level are the foundation for valid experiments and meaningful biological insights (35-45 minutes).
  5. Open Retrieval & Regulate to complete the self‑check and flag any item you’re unsure about—these notes will guide focused questions and discussions with your peers and instructor during lab. (5–10 min)
Deliverables before lab:
  • Completed scientific process questions.
  • Completed conversions set (all items in exponential form).
  • Microscope care and use.

Tip: If any concept felt unclear during your pre‑lab, write it down and bring it to lab. Clarifying those concepts with peers and your instructor will deepen your understanding and move you toward higher‑level thinking—an essential step in mastering biology

How This Connects to Next Week

All future labs depend on accurate measurement and reproducible observation. This week’s metrics and microscopy skills will be used to size cells, quantify change, and evaluate evidence—so nail the fundamentals now.
