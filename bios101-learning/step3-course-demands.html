Step 3 — Analyze BIOS101

Purpose

This step identifies what BIOS101 actually measures. Difficulty here is not about intelligence — it reflects a gap between current preparation and course demands. That gap is a “yet,” not a limit. I am not there “yet,” — and now I know exactly what ‘there’ requires.

Core Course Themes

  • Scientific Method & Experimental Design
  • Cellular Structures & Function
  • Macromolecules & Biochemistry Basics
  • Photosynthesis
  • Cell cycle, Mitosis, & Meiosis
  • Genetics

Skill Emphasis

  • Applying the scientific process
  • Converting metric measurements
  • Analyzing structure–function relationships
  • Identifying macromolecules
  • Interpreting biochemical and cellular processes
  • Analyzing chromosomal data

Learn the Lexicon: Master the Building Blocks of Biological Language.

Systematically identify parts of and compound biological words:

e.g. Suffixes (Endings)

‑ase (enzyme) | ‑ose (sugar/carbohydrate) | ‑lysis (breakdown) | ‑plast (organelle) | ‑phyll (leaf/pigment) | ‑logy (study of)

e.g. Prefixes (Beginnings)

chloro‑ (green) | cyto‑ (cell) | endo‑ (inside) | exo‑ (outside) | hyper‑ (above/excess) | hypo‑ (below/less) | iso‑ (equal) | poly‑ (many) | mono‑ (one)

e.g. Roots (Base Words)

bio (life) | spectro (light spectrum) | chrom (color) | nucleo (nucleus) | glyco (sugar)

Identify Likely Failure Points

  • Units & Conversions: nano, micro, milli; exponential notation
  • Variables:
    • Independent vs. Dependent vs. Control
    • Qualitative vs. Quantitative
  • Scientific Method: Hypothesis vs. Theory vs. Law
  • Cell Biology
    • prokaryotic vs eukaryotic
    • mitosis vs meiosis
    • meiosis I vs meiosis II
    • crossing over vs. Independent assortment
  • Biochemistry
    • Hydrolysis vs. Dehydration Synthesis
    • Mono- vs. Di- vs. Poly- (Saccharides/Mers)
    • Subunits vs. Macromolecules
    • Protein Structure: Primary vs. Secondary vs. Tertiary vs. Quaternary
  • Similar Terms:
    • Chromatin vs. chromatid
    • Microscopy:
  • Magnification vs. Resolution vs. Contrast
  • Course vs. fine focus

What BIOS101 Actually Tests

  • Recall + Application: definitions AND lab-based use
  • Procedural Thinking: logic of experimental design
  • Analytical Skills: graphs, ratios, predictions
  • Data Handling: metric conversions, Punnett squares
  • Integration: structure → function across levels

Memory & Thinking Demands

  • Encoding: organized categories and visual anchors
  • Retrieval: rapid recall under time pressure
  • Higher-Order Thinking: compare, explain, synthesize

Yet → There Alignment Check

“There” means being able to perform the skills BIOS101 actually tests — not just recognize terms or reread notes.

If you are not there yet, that does not indicate inability. It indicates that your current preparation methods are not aligned with course demands.

Identify your current “yet”:

  • I can recognize terms but struggle to apply them.
  • I understand examples but cannot transfer them to new questions.
  • I follow processes when shown but cannot reconstruct them independently.
  • I know formulas but hesitate under time pressure.

In the next step, you will design strategies that deliberately move you from “yet” toward “there.”
