# Scientific Theory: Water Molecule Structure

## Molecular Geometry

### Bond Length and Angle
The water molecule (H₂O) has a characteristic bent shape with:
- **O-H Bond Length**: 0.9572 Å (in gas phase)
- **H-O-H Bond Angle**: 104.52° (in gas phase)
- **Molecule Type**: V-shaped/bent molecular geometry

### Why the Bent Shape?
The bent shape arises from:
1. **Tetrahedral electron geometry**: Oxygen has 6 valence electrons
2. **Two bonding pairs**: Form O-H covalent bonds
3. **Two lone pairs**: On oxygen atom
4. **VSEPR theory prediction**: AX₂E₂ type molecule

## Polarity and Dipole Moment

### Electronegativity Difference
- **Oxygen electronegativity**: 3.44 (Pauling scale)
- **Hydrogen electronegativity**: 2.20 (Pauling scale)
- **Difference**: 1.24 → Polar covalent bonds

### Partial Charges
- **Oxygen**: Partial negative charge (δ⁻) of approximately -0.834 e
- **Hydrogen**: Partial positive charge (δ⁺) of approximately +0.417 e each
