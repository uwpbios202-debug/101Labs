Step 1: Word Study

⚠️ Experts don't skip unfamiliar terms — they interrogate them.
Understand these words before reading the background paragraphs.

Anabaena
Morpheme breakdown: Ana- (Greek ἀνά) means up, back, or again, and -baena (from Greek βαίνω, baínein) means to go, to walk, or to move.
The name reflects the organism’s visible movement and filamentous growth—not true locomotion, but the characteristic shifting observed under the microscope, especially in living wet mounts.
Prokaryotic
Morpheme breakdown: Pro- (before) + karyo- (nucleus) + -tic (relating to)
Cells that lack a membrane-bound nucleus and organelles. DNA floats freely in the cytoplasm. Includes bacteria and archaea.
Cyanobacterium
Morpheme breakdown: Cyano- (blue-green) + bacterium (single-celled microorganism)
Photosynthetic bacteria that obtain energy through photosynthesis. Often called "blue-green algae" but are actually bacteria, not algae.
Photosynthesis
Morpheme breakdown: Photo- (light) + synthesis (putting together)
The process by which organisms convert light energy (usually from the sun) into chemical energy stored in glucose, releasing oxygen as a byproduct.
Eukaryotic
Morpheme breakdown: Eu- (true) + karyo- (nucleus) + -tic (relating to)
Cells that contain a true, membrane-bound nucleus and other membrane-bound organelles. Includes animals, plants, fungi, and protists.
Organelles
Morpheme breakdown: Organ (functional structure) + -elle (small/diminutive)
Specialized structures within a cell that perform specific functions, such as mitochondria (energy production), nucleus (genetic control), and chloroplasts (photosynthesis in plants).

Anabaena (~2–10 µm) is a small, prokaryotic, filamentous cyanobacterium that performs photosynthesis, with some cells specialized for nitrogen fixation, and has no nucleus or organelles. In contrast, typical eukaryotic cells (~10–100 µm) are larger, have a true nucleus and organelles, and support more complex metabolism.

⚠️ Common Misconception Alert!

Filament ≠ Tissue

Anabaena forms a filament (a chain of individual cells), but this is NOT the same as tissue. Tissue is an organized group of cells working together in multicellular organisms (eukaryotes). Anabaena cells are connected but remain independent prokaryotic cells. Don't confuse structural connection with true multicellular organization!
