Qualitative vs. Quantitative Observations
Background Information: In type 2 diabetes, high blood-sugar levels eventually cause complications of the vascular system, kidneys, and other organs. Researchers at the Beltsville Human Nutrition Research Center in Maryland conducted an experiment to test if taking cinnamon every day had any effect on the blood glucose, fat, and cholesterol of patients with type 2 diabetes. Experimental Design: At the beginning of the experiment, the levels of blood glucose, fat, and cholesterol were measured for every patient. The researchers gave a total of 60 people with type 2 diabetes various amounts of cinnamon every day for 40 days. One group of 20 patients were given 1 g (1/4 tsp) of cinnamon; another group of 20 patients were given 3 g of cinnamon; and a third group of 20 patients were given 6 g of cinnamon. A second set of 60 people with type 2 diabetes were selected for the control group. These patients were given a placebo (wheat-flour) in amounts equivalent to the groups that received the cinnamon (1g, 3g, 6g). The placebo was also taken every day for 40 days. After the 40 days, researchers again measured the levels of blood glucose, fat, and cholesterol for every patient. The patients in the control group experienced no change in these three levels. The patients receiving the various amounts of cinnamon experience a drop in their blood glucose, fat, and cholesterol levels ranging from 18-29%.
