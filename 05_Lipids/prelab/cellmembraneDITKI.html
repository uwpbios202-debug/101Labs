Quick Reference Guide:

Phospholipids: Amphipathic molecules with glycerol backbone, 2 fatty acid tails (hydrophobic), and phosphate head (hydrophilic). They spontaneously form bilayers in aqueous solutions.

Cholesterol: Steroid molecule that fits between phospholipids. At low temperatures: prevents tight packing (maintains fluidity). At high temperatures: restrains movement (reduces fluidity).

Integral Proteins: Hydrophobic regions interact with fatty acid tails. Functions include: transport channels, receptors, enzymes, cell adhesion.

Peripheral Proteins: Attached to membrane surface via ionic bonds. Often anchor cytoskeleton or serve as enzymes.

Glycocalyx: Carbohydrate coating on extracellular surface. Functions: cell recognition, protection, adhesion, immune response.

ECM Fibers: Extracellular matrix provides structural support and cell signaling.

Cytoskeleton: Microfilaments (actin) provide mechanical support and help maintain cell shape.
